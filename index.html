Follow the Money

OpenAI just closed the largest private funding round in history at $122 billion. Amazon, Nvidia, and SoftBank led. But where does all that money actually go?

02 — Follow the Money

The Circular Cash Flow

A growing chorus of financial analysts have observed that OpenAI's two largest investors are also its two most critical infrastructure vendors. Here's how the money actually moves.

The Nvidia Loop
Nvidia invests $30B → OpenAI spends it on Nvidia hardware → Nvidia books it as revenue
1

Nvidia moves $30B off its balance sheet

Nvidia invests $30B in OpenAI's funding round as a mix of cash and compute capacity commitments. As of Q1 FY2026 (May 2025), Nvidia held $31.4B in cash and marketable securities.

Balance Sheet → Investment
2

OpenAI commits to 5GW of Nvidia systems

OpenAI secured 3GW of dedicated inference capacity and 2GW of training on Nvidia's upcoming Vera Rubin systems. Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang has said building 1GW of data center capacity costs ~$50B, of which ~$35B is Nvidia hardware.

OpenAI Cash → Nvidia Hardware
3

Nvidia records it as revenue

NewStreet Research estimated that for every $10B Nvidia invests in OpenAI, it will see $35B in GPU purchases or lease payments — about 27% of its annual revenue. OpenAI's GPU spend flows back to Nvidia's income statement as data center revenue (FY2025: $130.5B total revenue).

Investment → Revenue Loop
The Amazon Loop
Amazon invests $50B → OpenAI commits $100B on AWS → Amazon books it as cloud revenue
1

Amazon invests $50B in OpenAI equity

Starting with an initial $15B cash infusion. Amazon (FY2025: $716.9B total revenue, AWS: $128.7B) has the cash flow to support it — trailing twelve-month operating cash flow was $139.5B.

Balance Sheet → Investment
2

OpenAI commits $100B on AWS over 8 years

This expands an existing $38B deal. OpenAI will consume 2GW of Trainium capacity (Amazon's custom AI chips). AWS becomes the exclusive third-party cloud distributor for OpenAI Frontier.

OpenAI Cash → AWS Revenue
3

Amazon gets back 2× its investment as revenue

William Blair analysts estimated the $100B deal could add ~$17B/year in AWS revenue if spread evenly — roughly 11% of AWS's expected 2026 revenue. Amazon invested $50B to secure $100B in future cloud revenue.

Investment → Revenue Loop

04 — What the Analysts Say

Circular Financing Under the Microscope

The debate is fierce. Here are the key voices and their positions — drawn from public statements and published analysis.

“Nvidia invests $100 billion in OpenAI, which then OpenAI turns back and gives it back to Nvidia.”

— Bryn Talkington, Managing Partner, Requisite Capital Management (CNBC, Sep 2025)

Jensen Huang dismissed the circular narrative, calling critical media reports “nonsense” and stating Nvidia would invest “a great deal of money” in OpenAI.

— Jensen Huang, Nvidia CEO (Press conference, Taipei, Feb 2026)

Seaport analyst Jay Goldberg, who holds a rare Sell rating on Nvidia, called the investment relationship “very murky” and compared Nvidia's backing to a parental loan on a mortgage.

— Jay Goldberg, Seaport Global Securities (Yahoo Finance, Nov 2025)

The Bull Case

Janus Henderson called the wave of AI deals a “virtuous circle” that aligns suppliers, builders, and customers to meet real demand. Bernstein's Stacy Rasgon said there may be no better use of Nvidia's cash. Unlike dot-com round-tripping, physical assets (GPUs, data centers) are being built. OpenAI's revenue is real: 900M+ weekly users, $2B/month revenue, 50M+ subscribers.

The Bear Case

Michael Burry tweeted spreadsheets analyzing cash loops. Jim Chanos maintains short positions citing historical vendor-finance collapses. Aswath Damodaran (NYU Stern) warns that stripping away compute commitments and conditional tranches leaves a much smaller cash injection than headlines suggest. The EU competition staff has flagged circular spending risks (Mar 2026).

The Dot-Com Parallel

In the late 1990s, telecom equipment makers like Nortel, Lucent, and Cisco lent money to startups and telcos to buy their equipment. Before the 2001 bust, financing extended by Cisco and Nortel exceeded 10% of annual revenue. 33–80% of vendor loan portfolios went uncollected as customers failed.

Key Difference

Unlike dot-com revenue round-tripping (buying each other's ads), Nvidia and OpenAI are performing their core business functions: Nvidia sells chips, OpenAI needs chips. Revenue flows one direction (to Nvidia). But the scale is unprecedented: Nvidia's AI startup investments totaled ~$53B across 170 deals from 2020–2025.

05 — The Wider Web

It's Not Just Two Companies

The circularity extends far beyond Nvidia and Amazon. The entire AI ecosystem is a web of interlocking investments and purchasing commitments.

CoreWeave
$22.4B
OpenAI's committed spend on CoreWeave data center capacity through 2029. CoreWeave has purchased 250,000+ Nvidia GPUs (~$7.5B). Nvidia owns ~7% of CoreWeave and invested another $2B in January 2026. Nvidia also guaranteed to buy any unsold CoreWeave compute for 6 years.
Microsoft
$13B+
Microsoft's cumulative OpenAI investment. Azure remains the exclusive cloud provider for OpenAI's stateless APIs. Microsoft receives 20% of OpenAI's revenue under their partnership. Nvidia also invested $10B in Anthropic alongside Microsoft ($5B) in November 2025.
AMD
160M shares
AMD granted OpenAI warrants for up to 160M shares (~10% of AMD) in exchange for OpenAI deploying 6GW of AMD GPU hardware by 2030. Critics called this arrangement “even more roundabout” than the Nvidia deal because AMD is giving up equity while receiving only revenue.
06 — The Bottom Line

So, Is the Circular Claim Valid?

★

Mostly True, With Important Nuance

The core claim — that Nvidia and Amazon invest cash that flows right back to them as revenue — is substantiated by financial analysts, SEC filings, and the deal structures themselves. Here's the breakdown:

  • Amazon invests $50B → OpenAI commits $100B on AWS over 8 years. Amazon is effectively investing to secure 2× its investment back as guaranteed cloud revenue. William Blair estimated ~$17B/year in new AWS revenue.
  • Nvidia invests $30B → OpenAI committed to 5GW of Nvidia Vera Rubin systems. NewStreet Research estimated every $10B invested yields $35B in GPU purchases. OpenAI's infrastructure spending is overwhelmingly on Nvidia hardware.
  • Both companies are OpenAI's primary vendors. Amazon (via AWS) and Nvidia (via GPUs) are the two most critical infrastructure suppliers for OpenAI's business.
  • The circularity is widely recognized. Fortune, Bloomberg, The Register, Noah Smith (Noahpinion), and multiple Wall Street analysts have documented these cash loops in detail.

The nuance:

  • It's not pure round-tripping. Unlike dot-com-era ad swaps, actual physical assets are being built (GPUs, data centers). Revenue flows primarily one direction: to the hardware/cloud vendor.
  • Nvidia's $30B isn't entirely cash. Nvidia CEO Huang clarified the $100B figure was “never a commitment” — it was an “invitation.” Much of Nvidia's investment is structured as compute capacity, not pure cash equity.
  • Amazon's $35B of the $50B is contingent on OpenAI going public or achieving AGI. Only $15B is immediate.
  • OpenAI generates real revenue — $25B annualized as of February 2026 from 900M+ weekly users. This isn't a company with no customers propping up fake demand.

The economist Noah Smith framed it well: this is vendor financing, not round-tripping. The concern isn't that it's illegal or fraudulent — it's that it may indicate demand isn't as strong as the hype suggests, and the intertwined balance sheets amplify systemic risk if any link in the chain breaks.
